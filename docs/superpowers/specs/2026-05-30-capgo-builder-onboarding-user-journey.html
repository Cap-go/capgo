The proposed end-user experience

You ask. It conducts. You ship.

A developer tells their AI assistant "set up native builds for my app," and the Capgo MCP walks them — one calm step at a time — from a plain Capacitor project to a signed iOS or Android build running in the cloud. The AI does everything it can itself, and only ever asks the person to do the few things software can't: sign into Apple or Google, make a couple of decisions.

01 · Before you begin

What you need, and how it starts

What you bring

  • A Capacitor app on your machine (the AI auto-detects the app id from capacitor.config).
  • A Capgo account — or the AI will hand you the link to make one and grab an API key.
  • For iOS: an Apple Developer account. For Android: a Google Play developer account.
  • The Capgo MCP connected to your AI client (Claude, Cursor, etc.).

How you kick it off

Either way lands in the same place:

  • Just say it: "Help me set up Capgo native builds for iOS." The AI recognizes the intent and starts.
  • Or run the command: /mcp__capgo__onboard_capgo_builder — a discoverable slash command that tells the AI to begin.

You never need to memorize tool names or flags. You talk; the AI drives.

The feel we're aiming for

One thing at a timeYou're never handed a 12-item checklist. Exactly one ask per turn.
Plain languageNo flags, no JSON, no tool names. "Paste your key here," "tap approve."
Never lose progressStop anytime. Say "continue" tomorrow and it resumes exactly where you left off.
Verify, don't assumeIt checks your key really works with Apple before moving on — no false "done."
Your secrets stay yoursCredentials live on your machine; used during the build, never stored on Capgo's servers.
An escape hatch alwaysStuck? It surfaces a diagnosis, a manual command, and a "tell me when ready" pause.

04 · When things go sideways

The bumps — and how they feel

A guided flow is only as good as its recovery. Each of these is handled as a calm, single next step — never a dead end.

no account yet

You're not logged in

The very first thing the AI hits.

What you see: "Let's connect Capgo first — here's the link to grab an API key, paste it here." Then it continues automatically.
conflict

The app already exists

You (or a teammate) already created it in Capgo.

What you see: "I found an existing com.acme.app — reuse it, or recreate it fresh?" A one-tap decision; it syncs the right id and moves on.
missing platform

No iOS / Android folder

You haven't added the native platform yet.

What you see: "Your project has no ios/ folder yet — want me to run cap add ios for you?" Yes → it adds it and carries on.
apple limit

Certificate limit reached

Apple caps you at 2 distribution certificates.

What you see: a list of your certs with dates/usage and "revoke the unused one?" You choose; it never deletes without asking.
build failed

The cloud build errored

A compile or signing error came back.

What you see: the actual error in plain terms, the likely cause, a suggested fix, and "want me to retry?" — never a silent failure or a fake success.
interrupted

You closed the chat halfway

Life happened during the Apple step.

What you see (later): say "continue Capgo setup" and it picks up exactly where you left off — your progress was saved to disk after every step.

05 · The honest ledger

Your job vs. Capgo's job

Set expectations plainly: here is the complete list of what a human has to do — everything else is handled.

Your job

the few things software can't do
  • Paste a Capgo API key — once (or skip if already logged in).
  • iOS: create an App Store Connect API key in Apple's portal and bring back 3 values.
  • Android: approve one Google sign-in in your browser.
  • Make 1–2 quick decisions (create-new vs import; revoke an old cert).
  • Say "go" to start the build. That's it.

Capgo + AI's job

everything else, verified each step
  • Detect your project, app id, platform, and whether a build is needed.
  • Register the app; resolve any naming conflict.
  • iOS: verify the key, create/rotate certificates, create the provisioning profile.
  • Android: generate the keystore, provision GCP + Play, validate access.
  • Save credentials securely, request the build, watch it, hand you the link + QR.
